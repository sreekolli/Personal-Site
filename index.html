01 / Who I am

Builder by instinct. Investor by conviction. Connector by calling.

I've spent the last decade oscillating between the two sides of the startup table — building startups and investing in founders. I started on Wall Street as a tech investment banker at J.P. Morgan, helping run financial models for $50B+ of debt financings, then moved into venture capital at the Partnership Fund for New York City, where I worked on $100M+ in deals across fintech, insurtech, enterprise, digital health, and life sciences.

I had the founder bug. I co-founded Conduit, a private-markets platform that connected 10K+ founders and investors globally, and subsequently was on the founding team of Goody — which raised $32M+ from Index, NEA, SoftBank, and others. Our team was recently listed on the Inc. 5000 fastest-growing startups.

Outside of building and investing, I'm an acrylic floral painter, Bharatanatyam dancer, and aspiring yogi. I trained on the trapeze as a child, and most recently took up gardening with the family. The creative practice isn't a side quest — it trains the same muscle that makes an investor notice a great founder: pattern recognition, timing, and the willingness to be bold.

02 / Themes I'm excited about

Markets I keep coming back to.

Theme · 01

Private Markets.

$124T · great wealth transfer

We're in the midst of the Great Wealth Transfer — a $124T shift of wealth from Baby Boomers to the next generations, reshaping how capital gets allocated across public and private markets.

What will this mean for financial markets — and who builds the infrastructure that moves it?
Theme · 02

Longevity.

8.3B people · aging is a global epidemic

8.3 billion people in the world, and aging is a global epidemic. The market is deeply fragmented, the science is real, and the world is eager for solutions at scale.

How do we foster a healthier society — and price patient capital into the timelines biology actually runs on?
Theme · 03

Education.

K–∞ · a world that doesn't exist yet

In a rapidly evolving world, we need to prepare the children of today for a world of tomorrow that doesn't exist yet.

What does the future of education look like when every student has a tutor, a studio, and a research lab in their pocket?
Theme · 04

Women's Health.

4.1B women · studied since the 1960s

There are 4.1 billion women in the world, and most studies only emerged in the 1960s. From diagnosis to care, the system is still broken.

How do we help women get the care they need — and rebuild the full stack around them?
Theme · 05

Deep Tech.

∞ · robotics · AI · space

From robotics to AI infrastructure to space, the world of opportunity keeps expanding — and so does what we ask machines to do on our behalf.

What does our future look like as mankind — and who has the taste to build it responsibly?
